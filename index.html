The Crimson Sovereignty

"The Sun is a tyrant. I shall dethrone it."

Welcome, traveler. You have stepped out of the light and into the shadow of Count Varrick von Kaelen.

We are a structured, high-quality Dark Fantasy RP group in VRChat, set in 12th Century Germany. We are not a rabble; we are the Iron Horde, sworn to a single, sacred mission: the conquest of the Sun.

Our Philosophy

  • Strategy Over Slaughter: We focus on covert acquisition and strategic sabotage, not mindless deathmatch.
  • Absolute Hierarchy: Varrick's word is law. There is no political mess, only directed action.
  • Lore-Driven: Every event serves the 800-year-old plot to secure the Nine Runics.

The Sovereign's Code

Law of the Spire

DISCLAIMER: We operate on a 3-Strike System. Violations result in Exile. Zero tolerance for hate speech or vile acts.

  • Each Violation results in +1 Strike.
  • 3 Strikes results in Exile (Kick) from the Spire for 1 month.
  • If you return and receive another strike during your probationary month, the penalty escalates to a longer Exile or Permanent Erasure (Ban), depending on the severity.
  • Redemption: One strike will be removed from your record every 2 months of loyal, rule-abiding service.

ZERO TOLERANCE:
Using slurs or hate speech during events or within the Spire is strictly forbidden and may result in immediate Exile, Erasure, or Blacklisting.
Any discussion or sharing of inappropriate content is anathema to the Bloodline. These offenses result in an Instant Ban and Blacklist.

1. The Maturity of the Bloodline (Age 16+)

The age requirement for this Sovereignty is 16. Exceptions may be made for those who display wisdom beyond their years. If you possess the cunning to deceive us about your age and we do not suspect you are younger, you likely possess the maturity required to serve. Members already sworn to the Spire who are under 16 are exempt from this edict.

2. Unity of the Horde (Respect & Kindness)

Treat your fellow kin with respect. The Spire is a sanctuary for our kind to revel in our dark purpose, both in and out of roleplay. Discrimination and bigotry (homophobia, transphobia, racism, etc.) are the tools of the weak and will never be tolerated. Harassment of any sworn member is an act of treason.

3. Decorum of the Court (No Obscene Content)

While our lore touches upon dark themes (such as the Womb of Despair), do not speak of inappropriate activities or make excessive references that disturb the comfort of the Court. Posting obscene videos or images is forbidden. Keep such things outside the Spire's walls, especially given the younger initiates among us.

4. Obey the Chain of Command (Listen to Staff)

The Butlers (Admins) have full discretion over enforcing the Count's will. If a Butler commands you to cease an action, stop immediately. They exist to ensure the Edicts are upheld and that our conquests flow smoothly. You must also heed the Iron Core (Knights/Mods)â€”they are the enforcers of the rules and speak with the authority of the leadership.

5. Laws of the Aether (Discord ToS)

This is vital. Do not commit any act that would bring the wrath of the platform upon us. Follow Discord's Terms of Service. This includes no doxxing and no exchange of pirated materials.

6. Silence in the Ranks (No Personal Drama)

Do not bring personal feuds into the Spire. Doing so only spreads chaos among the Horde. If you have a grievance with another member, bring it quietly to a Butler, and do not drag the entire Sovereignty into your dispute. Speak to us if you have been made uncomfortable or disrespected, but do not make a spectacle of it.

7. The Spirit of the Edict (No Loopholes)

Do not attempt to outwit the law. Searching for loopholes will result in the same punishment as breaking the rule you sought to bypass. Use your common sense. If you suspect an action violates the spirit of the Count's law, DO NOT DO IT.

8. The Duty of Vigilance (Report Violations)

We cannot watch every shadow. We do not wish to hover over you like a paranoid jailer. However, the Edicts exist for the survival of the group. If you witness a breach of the law, you must inform us. Confidentiality is guaranteed. This is not an invitation to spy on your brethren, but a request to protect the integrity of the Spire. Proof is required: video for VRChat incidents, screenshots for Discord. Send this proof directly to a Butler or Officer.

9. The Joy of Conquest (HAVE FUN!)

The most vital rule: Enjoy the Darkness. We are strict when necessary, but our ultimate goal is the enjoyment of the narrative and the company of the Horde. As long as you follow these Edicts, you are safe under the Count's patronage. We are here to relax and conquer together. May you find a second family within these walls.

The Crimson Bible

I. The Origin (1120â€“1155 AD)

Before he was the Sovereign, he was Graf Varrick von Kaelen, a feared noble of the Black Forest. Betrayed by the Prince-Bishop of Mainz and chained to a spire at sunrise, he burned for six hours but refused to die. He vowed to consume the sun that tried to execute him.

II. The Unification of the Horde

The Orcs (Iron-Blood Clan): Betrayed mercenaries from the Harz Mountains. Varrick bought their loyalty with the heads of the Imperial Guard.

The Ogres (Alp-Walkers): Massive brutes tamed by Varrick's steady supply of food. Living siege engines.

The Goblins (Rhine-Rats): Masters of the Cologne sewers, saved from drowning by Varrick's ice magic. Spies and thieves.

The Deep Crawlers

The Brood Weavers (Net-Spinners): Monstrous, crystal-backed arachnids found in the deepest silver mines of the Black Forest. Varrick fed them runic-infused flesh, turning their webs into steel-hard traps. They serve as ambush predators.

The Experiments of Flesh

Rat Ogres (The Flesh-Molded): Created in Varrick's labs using alchemy stolen from the University of Heidelberg. Twisted mutations of man and beast, fueled by "Vitalis Glass." They are Shock Troops, unleashed to break enemy lines.

Dark Demonic Monsters: Creatures summoned from the "Hexen-Void," ancient pagan spirits bound to Varrick's will. They are used as Terror Weapons.

Key Locations for Roleplay

I. THE CARPATHIAN SPIRE (Headquarters)

The geo-magical anchor of the Bloodline. Safe territory for all sworn members.

The Prism Chamber (The Summit)

Description: The highest point of the Spire. It houses the Runic of Zenith and a massive, complex array of lenses and mirrors aimed at the sky. The walls are etched with star charts.

Atmosphere: Cold, silent, smelling of ozone and old parchment.

The Great Hearth (Social Hub)

Description: A cavernous, gothic dining hall lit by floating braziers of cold, blue fire. Long tables are set for the Horde, though the food varies (meat for Orcs, fine wine for Butlers).

Usage: General social RP, receiving mission briefings, and inter-faction mingling.

Atmosphere: Boisterous but disciplined. The only place where an Ogre and a Skinstealer might share a drink.

The Hollows (The Undercroft)

Description: A labyrinth of damp stone tunnels beneath the Spire. This is the domain of the Goblins and Mendacius. It contains the torture cells, the Skinstealer's "wardrobe," and the Goblin workshops.

Usage: Interrogation RP, stealth training, and darker character development.

Atmosphere: Claustrophobic, damp, and filled with the sounds of grinding metal and distant screams.

The Aerie of Ash (The Roost)

Description: Huge, open balconies jutting from the Spire's mid-section. This is where the Desmodus Bats and Dragons roost. The stone is scorched and stained.

Usage: Preparing for aerial sorties, caring for the beasts, and overlooking the valley below.

Atmosphere: Windy, exposed, and smelling of sulfur and raw meat.

II. THE SHADOWED DOMINION (Controlled Territory)

The lands immediately surrounding the Spire. Dangerous to mortals, safe for the Horde.

The Bone-Fields (The War Camp)

Description: A desolate, muddy plateau at the base of the Spire. It is littered with the skeletal remains of failed sieges. Here, the Orcs and Ogres drill constantly, building siege engines and wrestling for dominance.

Usage: Combat training, duels, and marshaling large armies for Saturday raids.

Atmosphere: Brutal, loud, and muddy.

The Blackwood Forest (The Perimeter)

Description: A dense, ancient pine forest that blocks the path to the Spire. The trees are twisted, and the mist never lifts. Holy Bulwarks patrol the tree line.

Usage: Patrol RP, hunting encroaching spies, and ambush setups.

Atmosphere: Eerie, silent, and intensely foggy.

The Ruined Chapel of St. Ilias (The Outpost)

Description: A desecrated Christian church halfway down the mountain. Varrick has converted it into a forward observation post. The altar is now a map table.

Usage: Small team briefings before a raid, lookout duty.

Atmosphere: Blasphemous and quiet. Stained glass is broken; religious icons are defaced.

III. THE CONTESTED FRONTIERS (Enemy Territory)

Hostile zones where the Horde must tread carefully. These are raid locations.

The Iron Pass (Iron Pact Territory)

Description: A narrow, fortified canyon controlled by the Iron Pact mercenaries. Stone watchtowers line the cliffs, and a massive gate blocks the trade road.

Usage: Sabotage Missions. Orcs attacking the gates while Goblins climb the cliffs to silence the watchtowers.

Danger Level: High. Heavily guarded by crossbowmen and boiling oil.

The Monastery of the White Sun (Inquisition Stronghold)

Description: A pristine, white-stone fortress located in a sunny valley. It is surrounded by fields of garlic and "Sun-Flowers" (magical flora that burns vampires). It houses a vast library of anti-undead knowledge.

Usage: Heist Missions. Stealing books, kidnapping priests for intel, or sabotaging their bells.

Danger Level: Extreme. The ground is consecrated; direct sunlight is amplified here.

The Silver Market of Buda (Neutral/Hostile City)

Description: The bustling medieval marketplace of a major human city. It is filled with merchants, spies, and the Silver Hand Cult alchemists.

Usage: Espionage & Infiltration. Skinstealers blending in to gather rumors, Goblins stealing reagents, and Butlers negotiating secret trades.

Danger Level: Variable. If cover is blown, the city guard swarms.

The Pale Crypts (Rival Vampire Territory)

Description: A subterranean necropolis controlled by the Cult of the Pale Moon. It is beautiful but deadly, filled with ancient traps and mental illusions.

Usage: Diplomacy or Assassination. Negotiating with rival vampires or raiding them for ancient artifacts.

Atmosphere: Silent, dusty, and filled with a sense of ancient, decaying nobility.

IV. SPECIAL/EVENT LOCATIONS

The Crash Site (The Meteor)

Description: A crater in the no-man's-land where a "Bleeding Star" fell. The earth is fused into glass, and the radiation interferes with magic.

Usage: Resource gathering events (mining Star-Metal).

The Floating Archipelago (The High Sanctum)

Description: Broken islands hovering in the sky, dripping waterfalls into the void. Accessible only by dragon-back or giant bat.

Usage: High-level raids against the Sky-Bound constructs.

The Mirror-Lake (The Gateway)

Description: A perfectly still, black lake deep in a cave. Looking into it reveals the "Mirror Realm."

Usage: Rituals involving the Night Walkers or summoning demons.

The Crossroads of the Hanging Man

Description: A muddy intersection of two main roads, marked by a gibbet where traitors are left.

Usage: Meeting contacts, exchanging prisoners, or executing the Count's Justice publicly.
